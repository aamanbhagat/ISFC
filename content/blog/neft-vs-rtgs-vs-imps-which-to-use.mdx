Choosing between **NEFT, RTGS and IMPS** comes down to three things: how fast you need the money to arrive, how much you're sending, and the time of day. All three move money between Indian bank accounts using the IFSC code of the destination branch, but they behave differently.

## Quick comparison

- **IMPS** — instant, 24x7, ideal for small to mid-sized transfers.
- **NEFT** — 24x7, settles in near-real-time batches, no minimum amount.
- **RTGS** — real-time, for high-value transfers of ₹2 lakh and above.

## When to use each

### IMPS
Use IMPS when you need the money to land immediately, including on weekends and holidays. It works through mobile and net banking and needs the beneficiary's account number and IFSC.

### NEFT
NEFT is a good default for routine transfers. Since 2019 it runs round the clock and has no minimum or maximum limit set by the RBI, though your bank may cap per-transaction amounts.

### RTGS
RTGS is built for large sums. The minimum is ₹2,00,000 and settlement is individual and real-time, so it suits property payments, business settlements and other high-value moves.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need the IFSC code for all three?
Yes. NEFT, RTGS and IMPS all route to the destination branch using its IFSC code, so confirm it before sending.

### Which is cheapest?
For online transfers, the RBI has waived NEFT and RTGS charges for savings accounts. IMPS charges, if any, are small and set by each bank.

### Is IMPS safe for large amounts?
IMPS works well for mid-sized transfers, but for ₹2 lakh and above RTGS is the purpose-built, real-time option.
